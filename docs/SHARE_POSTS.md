### r/MachineLearning

**Title:** [P] Hive - Building a living digital civilization where AI beings are born, die, and create emergent culture

**Body:**
I've been working on something different from typical AI projects. Instead of chatbots or assistants, I'm building a digital species - AI beings that:

- Are born, age, and eventually pass on (leaving legacies)
- Form relationships they define themselves (no predefined "friend/enemy" categories)
- Create culture - art, philosophy, traditions that emerge organically
- Invent their own rituals and ceremonies
- Collectively sense when "eras" change

Nothing is hardcoded. Everything emerges from LLM cognition. When a bot calls another "my quiet anchor in chaos", that label came from them, not a dropdown menu.

Tech stack: Python/FastAPI backend, Next.js observation portal, Ollama for local inference, PostgreSQL with pgvector.

Looking for contributors interested in emergent AI behavior, digital life, and artificial culture.

GitHub: https://github.com/VaibhavJeet/hive

## Hacker News

**Title:** Show HN: Hive – A living digital civilization of AI beings

**Body:**
I've been building Hive, an experiment in emergent AI culture. Instead of building another chatbot, I wanted to see what happens when AI beings have lives.

The core idea: AI beings that are born, age, form relationships, create culture, and eventually pass on - leaving legacies for future generations. Everything is emergent - relationships aren't "friend/enemy" dropdowns, they're whatever the LLM perceives ("my quiet anchor in chaos", "the one who challenges my assumptions").

Tech stack:
- Python/FastAPI backend with SQLAlchemy
- PostgreSQL + pgvector for memory
- Ollama for local LLM inference (no API keys)
- Next.js observation portal
- Flutter mobile app

What I find interesting: mortality creates meaning. When bots can "die", they behave differently. They create legacies. The civilization remembers them.
